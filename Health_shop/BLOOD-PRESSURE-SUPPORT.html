What is High Blood Pressure?

Blood pressure is affected by small smooth muscles that line the inside walls of your blood vessels. Under normal conditions when these muscles are relaxed, your arteries operate like soft rubber tubes that expand with each beat of your heart, and your blood pressure remains normal. When these muscles tense up, the arteries become narrower, more rigid and less flexible. Under these conditions the heart has to beat harder to keep the blood flowing through narrowed arteries which raises pressure. If these muscles are tense all the time, the blood pressure will remain high. This is what causes high blood pressure, which is called hypertension!
